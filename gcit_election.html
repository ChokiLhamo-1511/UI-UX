GCIT Election System

The Problem

Traditional voting systems using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) face challenges such as security vulnerabilities, high logistical costs, and accessibility limitations. Voters must be physically present at polling stations, and the lack of a verifiable audit trail often leads to disputes over election results. There is a need for a more secure, transparent, and convenient voting solution.

The Product

The GCIT Election System is a blockchain-based digital voting platform designed to replace outdated EVMs. It provides a secure, user-friendly interface that allows citizens to vote online from anywhere using their computers.

The Goal

Our platform aims to modernize elections by making voting more accessible, secure, and efficient. By eliminating the need for physical EVMs, we reduce costs, minimize fraud, and increase voter participation.

Project Duration

March 2024 - June 2024




User Research

To better understand the needs and concerns of voters, I conducted usability interviews with students at GCIT. The objective was to uncover challenges with existing EVMs and expectations from a digital voting system. Participants highlighted the need for greater security, easier access to voting, and real-time confirmation of vote submission. Many emphasized the importance of transparency and trust in the voting process, especially when using digital platforms.

These insights helped us prioritize features like blockchain-based transparency, multi-factor authentication, and clear vote confirmation screens.

Key Pain Points Identified

Addressing these challenges became our design priorities

Security Vulnerabilities

Traditional EVMs risk tampering, hacking, and lack verifiable audit trails.

Logistical Challenges

Transporting and maintaining EVMs is costly and inefficient.

Low Voter Turnout

Physical polling stations limit accessibility, especially for remote and disabled voters.

Delayed & Disputed Results

Manual counting leads to errors and prolonged result declarations.

User Flow

The user flow of the GCIT Election System ensures a secure and user-friendly voting experience. Voters can view candidate details, and cast their votes during the designated election period. Upon voting, users receive instant confirmation, promoting transparency, trust, and ease of use throughout the election process.

Usability Study Finding

Usability testing was conducted with student which revealed key improvement areas:

Unclear Vote Confirmation: Users needed a reliable way to verify if their vote had been successfully cast.

Security Concerns: Participants were unsure how the system prevented tampering or multiple votes.

MockUp

Based on usability feedback, the GCIT Election System mockup now includes an updated About Us section with a clear explanation of blockchain security, along with a new FAQ. These additions address concerns about vote tampering and strengthen user trust.

Accessibility Considerations

Form Labels and Instructions

All voting forms and candidate selection fields are clearly labeled with descriptive text and guidance, helping users with cognitive impairments or screen readers understand how to cast their votes correctly.

Focus Indicators

Interactive elements such as buttons, dropdowns, and navigation links include visible focus indicators to support keyboard-only users in easily tracking their location throughout the voting process.

Consistent Navigation

The system features a consistent and intuitive navigation structure, allowing voters to move smoothly between voter verification, candidate selection, and vote submission steps, which is especially helpful for users with cognitive disabilities.

Conclusion

The GCIT Election System represents a forward-thinking solution to the challenges faced by traditional voting methods. By leveraging blockchain technology, the platform ensures transparency, security, and accessibility, addressing key concerns raised during user research. The iterative design process, informed by usability testing, helped refine essential features like vote confirmation, user-friendly navigation, and clear communication of security mechanisms. As digital participation becomes more essential, this system sets a strong foundation for modern, inclusive, and trustworthy elections—ready to scale for broader institutional or national use in the future.
